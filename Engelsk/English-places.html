Intramuros. Manila

Intramuros comprises a centuries-old historic district, entirely surrounded by fortifications, that was considered at the time of the Spanish Empire to be the entire City of Manila.
Intramuros served as the seat of government of the Captaincy General of the Philippines, a component realm of the Spanish Empire, housing the colony's governor-general from its founding in 1571 until 1865, and the Real Audiencia of Manila until the end of Spanish rule during the Philippine Revolution of 1898. The walled city was also considered the religious and educational center of the Spanish East Indies.

Vigan

Located on the western coast of the large island of Luzon, facing the South China Sea, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and it is one of the few Spanish colonial towns left in the Philippines whose old structures have mostly remained intact. It is well known for its sett pavements and a unique architecture of the Spanish Philippines colonial era which fuses native Philippine and Oriental building designs and construction, with colonial Spanish architecture that is still abundant in the area, mainly the bahay na bato houses and an Earthquake Baroque church.

San Agustin Church. Manila

Located in the historic district of Intramuros in Manila, San Agustin Church is one of the oldest and most significant churches in the Philippines. Built in 1607, it is considered one of the gems of Spanish colonial architecture and is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. San Agustin is not only a religious site but also a symbol of the country's rich history and culture.

Festivals of the Philippines


Sinulog Festival


Sinulog Festival one of the biggest and most vivid holidays in the Philippines, that gather more than 1 million participants every year. It is traditional holiday and held in Cebu City in honour of the Santo Niño(Child Jesus). It gathers attention not only locals but tourists from all over the world. Sinulog has special meaning in the Catholic tradition because it is symbolising conversion to Christianity. Sinulog is a holiday that unites history and faith, culture and traditions.

The word “Sinulog” comes from the Cebuano word sulog, meaning “like water current.” It refers to the dance's signature forward-and-backward steps, said to mimic the movement of flowing water. This rhythmic dance is the heartbeat of the festival.

It held Every 3rd Sunday of January.

Ati-Atihan Festival


The name Ati-Atihan literally means “to be like the Ati” — the indigenous people of Panay Island. During the celebration, locals and visitors smear their faces with soot or black paint to honor the Atis, then don colorful headdresses and costumes. The streets erupt with the hypnotic beat of drums, chants, and dancing that doesn't just happen on stage — it happens everywhere, with everyone.

The Ati-Atihan Festival is an annual religious and cultural celebration in Kalibo, the capital of Aklan Province in the Philippines. The festival is held in honour of the Santo Niño, the Infant Jesus, and is celebrated in several towns of the province of Aklan, particularly in Kalibo. The festival is believed to be around 800 years old and is considered the mother of all Philippine festivals.

It held in Januar (same week as Sinulog).

Panagbenga Festival


The Panagbenga Festival, also known as the Baguio Flower Festival, is an annual event celebrated in the vibrant city of Baguio in the Philippines. Celebrated every February, this month-long festival showcases the rich floral heritage and colourful culture of the city. In the native Kankanaey language, Panagbenga means "season of blooming," symbolizing the resilience and creativity of Baguio. It attracts thousands of visitors from around the world with its beautiful displays of flowers, vibrant parades, and rich cultural performances that bring the city to life

The Panagbenga Festival stands as a testament to the Filipino spirit of resilience, creativity, and community. From its humble beginnings as a post-disaster recovery initiative to its current status as one of Asia's most celebrated cultural events, the festival continues to bloom and evolve. It reminds us that like the flowers it celebrates, beauty can emerge from adversity, and with proper nurturing, can flourish into something that brings joy and inspiration to millions.

It held in Februar
