Political System

Political system According to the 1987 Constitution, the Philippines is a presidential republic with a bicameral parliament and an independent judiciary. The head of state is the president, who is elected by direct universal suffrage for six-year term. The highest legislative body in the country is Congress, which consists of two chambers: the Senate of the Philippines (24 seats) and the House of Representatives of the Philippines (316 seats).
Corruption is a usual thing (some of historians think that it came from Spanish colonial period).
