Philippines national symbols


State Motto: Maka-Diyos, Maka-tao, Makakalikasan, at Makabansa ("For God, People, Nature, and Country")


Anthem - Lupang Hinirang
Chosen Land

The Philippine eagle was officially declared the national bird of the Philippines on July 4, 1995, by President Fidel V. Ramos under Proclamation No. 615, series of 1995. Due to the eagle's size and rarity, it is also a coveted species for birdwatchers
The South Sea pearl or Philippine pearl was declared by Philippine President Fidel Ramos as the national gem in 1996 through Proclamation No. 905. The oyster and pearl are depicted on the reverse side of the Philippine New Generation Currency Series 1,000-peso bill.
The Philippines adopted the sampaguita (Arabian jasmine, Jasminum sambac) in 1934 as its national flower because it symbolizes purity and cleanliness due to its color and sweet smell. It is popularly strung into garlands presented to visitors and dignitaries and is a common adornment on religious images.
In 2009, Arnis was declared the national martial art and sport of the Philippines, a recognition of its cultural importance and its role in promoting Filipino identity. Today, Arnis practitioners can be found around the globe, celebrating its heritage while adapting its techniques to modern self-defense and fitness.
